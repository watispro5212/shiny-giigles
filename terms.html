Protocol Directives (Terms of Service)

DOCUMENT CLASSIFICATION // OMEGA-1 // 2026.03.16

01. Authorized Access & Conduct

Operatives granted access to the Nexus Protocol are bound by strict conduct matrices. Malicious exploits, unauthorized server penetration (DDoS, phishing), or attempting to bypass the credit-generation cooling cycles via automated bot-nets will result in an immediate global firewall ban and asset freezing.

02. Virtual Assets (NXC)

Nexus Credits (NXC) are encrypted tokens representing digital influence within the protocol. They hold strictly zero real-world fiat currency value. They are non-transferable outside the Nexus ecosystem, and any attempt to exchange NXC for external currencies is a violation of the primary directive.

03. Node Administrator Liability

Node Administrators (Server Owners) who deploy the Nexus Protocol are exclusively responsible for actions taken via its subroutines in their domain. Command executions such as /purge or /ban must be configured with precise permission hashes. The core architectural team is not liable for accidental data wipes or misconfigured Black-Ice (AutoMod) settings.

04. Uplink Availability

As a community-driven superstructure, Nexus access is provided "as-is." While our ShardingManager architecture targets a 99.98% global uplink stability, interruptions during emergency maintenance cycles or localized sector outages are possible and do not warrant asset compensation.
